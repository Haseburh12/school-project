Phishing Basics

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers use deceptive techniques to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. Phishing attacks typically involve sending fraudulent emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources, such as banks, social media platforms, or government agencies.

Here are some common characteristics of phishing attacks:

  • Spoofed Sender: Phishing emails often appear to be from trusted entities, but the sender's email address is usually fake or manipulated to deceive recipients.
  • Sense of Urgency: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency to prompt recipients to take immediate action, such as clicking on a link or providing sensitive information.
  • Request for Personal Information: Phishing emails may request sensitive information, such as passwords, account numbers, or social security numbers, under the guise of a legitimate inquiry or security update.
  • Malicious Links or Attachments: Phishing emails often contain malicious links or attachments that, when clicked or opened, can install malware on the recipient's device or redirect them to a fake website designed to steal their information.

It's important to remain vigilant and cautious when interacting with emails, messages, or websites, especially if they request sensitive information or exhibit suspicious behavior. By recognizing the signs of phishing attacks and taking appropriate precautions, individuals can protect themselves from falling victim to cybercriminals.

How to Recognize Phishing

Protecting Yourself from Phishing

  • Be cautious of unexpected emails, especially those requesting sensitive information or urging immediate action.
  • Verify the authenticity of emails and websites by checking for spelling errors, suspicious links, and unusual sender addresses.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for your accounts to add an extra layer of security.
  • Regularly update your software, including operating systems, browsers, and antivirus programs, to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown or untrusted sources.
  • When in doubt, contact the organization directly using trusted contact information to verify the legitimacy of a request.
